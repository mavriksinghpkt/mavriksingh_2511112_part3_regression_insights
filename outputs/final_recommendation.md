### Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

Based on the regression analysis, several factors were found to be associated with monthly sales performance across stores. The results indicate that customer footfall, marketing spend, and inventory availability are among the strongest drivers of monthly sales.

The multiple regression model provided the most comprehensive understanding of sales performance and was therefore selected as the final model for business decision-making.

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

The regression analysis suggests that the following factors are most strongly associated with monthly sales:

* Customer footfall
* Marketing spend
* Inventory availability percentage

These variables demonstrated strong relationships with monthly sales and were among the most statistically significant predictors in the regression models.

## Variables Leadership Should Focus On

Leadership should prioritize the following areas:

# 1. Customer Footfall

Increasing customer visits should remain a key strategic objective. Actions such as local marketing campaigns, loyalty programs, promotional activities, and improved customer experience may help increase footfall and ultimately improve sales.

# 2. Marketing Investment

Marketing expenditure showed a positive relationship with sales. Leadership should continue investing in marketing activities while monitoring return on investment to ensure spending remains effective.

# 3. Inventory Availability

Maintaining high inventory availability is critical. Stock shortages may lead to lost sales opportunities, while improved inventory management can directly support revenue growth.

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Variables with weak statistical significance or high p-values should be interpreted cautiously.

Differences across certain regions or store types may not always represent meaningful business effects and may instead reflect random variation within the data.

Therefore, leadership should avoid making major strategic decisions based solely on statistically weak variables.

## Recommended Business Actions

Based on the regression findings, the following actions are recommended:

* Increase initiatives designed to improve customer footfall.
* Continue targeted marketing investments and regularly evaluate campaign effectiveness.
* Strengthen inventory planning processes to reduce stock-outs.
* Monitor store performance by region and store type to identify high-performing segments.
* Periodically update the regression model as new data becomes available.

## Risks and Limitations

Leadership should keep the following limitations in mind:

* The regression model does not capture all factors affecting sales.
* External influences such as competition, seasonality, economic conditions, and customer preferences were not explicitly included.
* Some variables may exhibit weak statistical significance.
* The model may under-predict or over-predict sales for certain stores.

Consequently, regression results should be used alongside managerial judgement and additional business analysis.

## Association Does Not Necessarily Mean Causation

Regression analysis identifies associations between variables but does not automatically prove cause-and-effect relationships.

For example, a positive relationship between marketing spend and sales does not necessarily mean that increased marketing alone caused higher sales. Other factors may influence both variables simultaneously.

Therefore, regression findings should be interpreted as evidence of association rather than definitive proof of causation. Additional experimentation, controlled testing, and business validation are required before making major strategic decisions.
