## Task 1: Dataset Understanding

# Objective

The objective of this project is to identify the business factors that influence **Monthly Sales** across retail stores using regression analysis. The findings will help management make informed decisions regarding marketing, staffing, inventory, discount strategies, and store operations.

1. Dependent Variable

The dependent (target) variable selected for this analysis is:

* monthly_sales

This variable represents the total monthly sales generated by each store and is the value that the regression model will predict.

---

# 2. Potential Independent Variables

The following variables are considered potential predictors of monthly sales:

* **marketing_spend** – Marketing expenditure for the store.
* **footfall** – Number of customers visiting the store.
* **avg_discount_pct** – Average discount percentage offered.
* **staff_count** – Number of employees working in the store.
* **inventory_availability_pct** – Percentage of inventory available for customers.
* **competitor_distance_km** – Distance to the nearest competitor.
* **holiday_flag** – Indicates whether the month contains a holiday period (0 = No, 1 = Yes).
* **customer_rating** – Average customer satisfaction rating.
* **region** – Geographic region where the store is located.
* **store_type** – Category of the store (e.g., Residential, High Street).

* **region** – Requires encoding (One-Hot Encoding) before regression.

# 6. Variables That May Not Be Useful for Regression

* **store_id** – Acts only as a unique identifier and does not explain sales performance.
* **monthly_profit** – Not suitable as an independent variable because it is another business outcome closely related to monthly sales. Using it to predict sales may introduce data leakage and reduce the validity of the regression model.

### Retail Sales Regression Analysis

## Business Problem Summary

The leadership team of a retail chain wants to understand which factors influence monthly sales performance across stores. The objective of this project is to identify the variables that are most strongly associated with monthly sales using regression analysis and provide business recommendations that can support strategic decision-making.

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains monthly performance information for multiple retail stores. It includes sales, profit, marketing expenditure, customer footfall, discounts, inventory availability, staffing levels, customer ratings, regional information, and store characteristics.

The dataset consists of both numerical and categorical variables and is suitable for regression analysis.

## Dependent and Independent Variables

# Dependent Variable

* `monthly_sales`

# Independent Variables

* `marketing_spend`
* `footfall`
* `avg_discount_pct`
* `inventory_availability_pct`
* `customer_rating`
* `staff_count`
* `competitor_distance_km`
* `holiday_flag`
* `region`
* `store_type`

## Regression Approach

Both simple and multiple linear regression models were developed.

* Simple regression models were used to evaluate the effect of one independent variable on monthly sales at a time.
* Multiple regression analysis was used to examine the combined impact of several predictors on monthly sales.

The dependent variable for all models was `monthly_sales`.

## Dummy Variable Approach

Categorical variables such as `region` and `store_type` were converted into dummy variables using one-hot encoding.

To avoid the dummy variable trap and redundancy, one category from each variable was excluded and used as the reference category.

## Model Comparison Summary

The simple regression models provided insight into the individual impact of variables such as marketing spend and customer footfall.

The multiple regression model demonstrated higher explanatory power because it considered several predictors simultaneously. It provided a more comprehensive understanding of store sales performance.

## Final Model Selected

The multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it explained a greater proportion of the variation in monthly sales and offered stronger business insights.

## Business Recommendation

The analysis indicates that customer footfall, marketing spend, and inventory availability are among the most important factors associated with monthly sales.

Management should focus on:

* Increasing customer footfall.
* Optimizing marketing investments.
* Improving inventory availability.
* Monitoring performance across regions and store types.

## Assumptions and Limitations

# Assumptions

* Linear relationships exist between predictors and monthly sales.
* The observations are independent.
* The regression errors are randomly distributed.
* Predictor variables are measured accurately.

# Limitations

* Regression identifies associations but does not prove causation.
* External factors such as economic conditions and seasonality were not included.
* Some variables may have weak statistical significance.
* The model may under-predict or over-predict certain stores.
