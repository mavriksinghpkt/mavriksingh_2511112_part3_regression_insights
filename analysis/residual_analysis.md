# Residual Analysis

## Introduction

Residual analysis was conducted to evaluate the performance of the final multiple regression model. Predicted monthly sales values were generated using the regression equation, and residuals were calculated as:

**Residual = Actual Monthly Sales − Predicted Monthly Sales**

Positive residual values indicate that actual sales exceeded predicted sales, whereas negative residual values indicate that actual sales were lower than predicted.

## Largest Positive Residuals

The records with the largest positive residuals represent stores that performed significantly better than predicted by the model. These observations suggest that certain factors influencing sales were not fully captured by the regression model.

Possible business explanations include:

* Strong local demand in specific regions.
* Successful promotional campaigns.
* Seasonal events or festivals.
* Superior customer service and store management.
* Other external influences not included in the model.

These stores indicate potential under-prediction by the model.

## Largest Negative Residuals

The records with the largest negative residuals represent stores whose actual sales were substantially lower than predicted.

Possible business explanations include:

* Stock shortages or inventory issues.
* Temporary operational disruptions.
* Increased local competition.
* Lower customer demand.
* Regional economic conditions affecting purchasing behaviour.

These observations suggest that the model may be over-predicting sales for some stores.

## Under-Prediction and Over-Prediction Assessment

The residual analysis indicates that the model does not predict every store with the same level of accuracy.

Stores with consistently positive residuals appear to be under-predicted, indicating that additional explanatory variables may be required to fully capture their sales performance.

Stores with consistently negative residuals appear to be over-predicted, suggesting that factors such as competition, seasonality, operational efficiency, and local market conditions should be incorporated into future models.

## Overall Conclusion

Overall, the multiple regression model provides useful sales predictions and explains a significant portion of the variation in monthly sales. However, residual analysis demonstrates that additional business factors could further improve prediction accuracy and model performance.
